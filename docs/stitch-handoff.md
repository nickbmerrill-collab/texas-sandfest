# Google Stitch Handoff

The frontend now has one intended "wow" motion feature: a living tide and crowd-flow canvas layered over the hero image.

## Motion Concept

- Animated Gulf tide ribbons move across the SandFest beach hero.
- Small sand-light particles drift and brighten around pointer movement.
- The effect sits behind text and above the photo overlay, so the brand remains readable.
- It respects `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- A compact "Motion OS" panel explains the animation as live tide, crowd, and heat signals.
- The panel includes a pause/resume control so the feature feels intentional and demo-safe.

## Stitch Direction

When running this through Google Stitch, keep the experience grounded in SandFest operations rather than making a generic landing page:

- First viewport: Texas SandFest brand, date, beach image, animated tide/crowd signal.
- Keep the "Motion OS" concept visible in the hero; it is the signature feature.
- Customer side: schedule, map, tickets, accessibility, Ask Sandy.
- Admin side: command status, ingestion pipeline, incidents, partners, finance, setup.
- Visual tone: beach operations, civic event, sponsor-ready, not a dark SaaS dashboard.

## Do Not Lose

- Local scraped media manifest as the asset source.
- Customer/admin split.
- Ingestion cockpit.
- QuickBooks credential-ready state.
- Motion layer as a purposeful event-status metaphor, not decorative noise.
