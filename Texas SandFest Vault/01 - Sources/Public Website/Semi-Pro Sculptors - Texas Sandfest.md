Semi-Pro Sculptors These artists' journey has not just begun, but their skills continue to strengthen with each carving of a sandcastle they create and each competition in which they spend time building out an artistry of sand. You won't want to miss watching these semi-pro artists transform into excellence right before your eyes with unbelievable talent create sand sculptures worth remembering. Darrell O'Connor I live in Wilmington Delaware, but for the last 40 years I’ve spent summer weekends building sandcastles at Dewey Beach. My journey into sand sculpting began in the mid 1980’s when I entered my first contest at Rehoboth Beach. While I didn’t place in that first competition, I continued practicing and refining my skills, mostly through trial and error, with the occasional “how-to” book for guidance. The practice paid off and I went on to win the Rehoboth contest nine times. Everything changed in 2014 when I entered my first advanced amateur contest in Wildwood New Jersey. There I was fortunate enough to meet a master sand sculptor, and our friendship became a turning point in the quality of my work.
