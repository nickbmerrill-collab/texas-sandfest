14. Port Aransas Community 
IndexPoints of Interest
1. Port Aransas Museum/Community Center  The 
Port Aransas Museum, run by the Port Aransas 
Preservation and Historical Association, is dedicated to 
preserving and showcasing the town’s history. The 
Community Center is home to a variety of local events.
2. Roberts Point Park/Patsy Jones Amphitheater  
Roberts Point Park is a 50-acre park perfect for waterfront 
family fun. Roberts Point forms a safe harbor for the 
municipal marina that boasts 250 boat slips. The Patsy 
Jones Amphitheater located inside the park is a lively 
outdoor music venue.
3. Port Aransas Welcome Center  Stop by the 
Welcome Center, where knowledgeable locals will help 
you discover the original island life of Port Aransas & 
Mustang Island.
4. City Hall, Library, & Civic Center  The Port Aransas 
City Hall, Ellis Memorial Library, and Civic Center serve as 
a hub of government and community activity in Port A.  
5. Port Aransas Nature Preserve at Charlie’s 
Pasture  Charlie’s Pasture is a vast, 1280-acre property 
with uplands, sand flats, marshes, wooded motts, and 
ponds that support a diversity of plants and animals. 
Hundreds of species of shorebirds, waterfowl, and 
songbirds use the area to forage, nest, rest, and refuel 
while undertaking strenuous migrations. 
6. Aransas Pass (Lydia Ann) Lighthouse  The Lydia 
Ann Lighthouse guided ships through the pass from 1857 
to 1954. Today, lighthouse grounds are private property, 
but you can catch a great view from a kayak or boat tour.
7. San José Island San José Island, also called St. Jo’s, is 
a privately-owned island just a short boat ride away. 
Historically a ranch, today the island is managed 
principally for wildlife. Hop aboard the jetty boat and ride 
across the channel to the island’s beach for a wealth of 
birding, shelling, fishing, and beachcombing. 
8. Amos Rehabilitation Keep/University of Texas 
Marine Science Institute  The University of Texas at 
Austin Marine Science Institute (UTMSI) is the oldest and 
most significant marine research facility on the Texas 
coast. On the campus is the Patton Center for Marine 
Science Education, which is a free educational 
walkthrough of eight different aquaria and interactive 
displays that are perfect for people of all ages who want 
to learn more about the local marine wildlife. The Amos 
Rehabilitation Keep, or ARK, rehabilitates marine turtles 
and birds from the coastal zone of Mustang Island and St. 
Jo’s Island. 
9. Horace Caldwell Pier Extending over 1,200 feet into 
the Gulf, Horace Caldwell Pier is a prime spot for catching 
Redfish, Black Drum, Sea Trout, Sharks, King Mackerel, and 
more. Pier entry subject to a small fee. Bait, tackle, and 
equipment are available for sale in the Keepers at Horace 
Caldwell Pier shop.
10. Farley Boat Works  Experience history brought to life 
at Farley Boat Works, where boatbuilders carry on the 
tradition of constructing wooden skiffs for tarpon fishing 
that was started by the Farley family in 1915.
11. Leonabelle Turnbull Birding Center  The birding 
center is a stop on the Great Texas Birding Trail that 
provides an up-close look at life in a wetland and 
panoramic views of the marsh. The center features a 
native pollinator garden, picnic tables, an observation 
tower, a 750-foot boardwalk, and a walking trail. 
12. Community Park, Pool, & More  Community park 
spaces include a public pool, skate park, and dog park. 
Full-sized basketball courts, baseball fields, and soccer 
fields make for ideal sport practice areas. Plus, there’s a 
chipping green, disc golf, shuffleboard, picnic spaces, and 
more.
13. Joan & Scott Holt Paradise Pond Paradise Pond is 
one of only a few wooded natural freshwater ponds on 
Mustang Island. Short boardwalks and seating areas 
provide excellent access to lush vegetation for hundreds 
of species of migrating songbirds in the spring and fall.
14. Port Aransas Community Theatre (PACT)  Catch 
a live show at the local theatre! PACT is a nonprofit 
organization providing quality entertainment that 
highlights local talent of all ages.
15. Port Aransas Art Center  The Art Center offers art 
classes for all ages, monthly art exhibits, and a wide 
range of activities. Visit the gallery to find art, 
photography, pottery, jewelry, and so much more!
